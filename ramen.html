Babish's Tonkotsu Ramen

Recipe by Binging with Babish

Description

This recipe is the product of the diligent testing and persistent love of food courtesy of Andrew Rea, a.k.a. "Babish" from Binging with Babish. If you're looking for a quintessentially rich pork broth ramen, look no further!

Ingredients

For the marinated soft boiled eggs:

  • Soft boiled eggs
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin

For the Tare:

  • Vegetable oil
  • Dried anchovies
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Bonito flakes
  • Sake

For the Tonkotsu Broth

  • 4 lbs pigs feet, cut into 1 inch thick pieces
  • 1 onion, cut into quarters
  • 6-8 scallions, chopped roughly
  • 3-inch knob of french ginger, roughly chopped

For the Homemade Ramen Noodles

  • Baking soda
  • All purpose flour
  • Bread flour

For the Chashu Pork

  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 knob ginger
  • 2 green onions
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 1/2 cup plain white sugar
  • 1 piece of pork belly

For Ramen Assembly

  • Sliced chashu pork
  • Cooked ramen noodles
  • Tare
  • Tonkotsu broth
  • Nori (dried seaweed)
  • Scallions
  • Menma

Method

For Marinated Soft Boiled Eggs

  1. Take some soft boiled eggs and shock them in an ice bath. Once you’ve done that peel the eggs and prepare your marinade.
  2. For your marinade you’re going to combine equal parts soy sauce and mirin. For every part soy sauce and mirin you add, you’re going to add 4 parts water. Mix to combine.
  3. Add your eggs to the marinade. They are going to float but just make sure there is enough marinade in your bowl to theoretically cover them.
  4. Cover them with plastic wrap and place in the fridge for at least 2 hours and up to 24 hours.
